Yale — Short Answer 2 (Draft 10)

Maya Chen · Common App supplement · 2026 cycle
Prompt What is something about which you have changed your mind in the last three years? What changed it?
Yale Short Answer 2 · 125 words max

I used to think identity was a thing you pickedchose. My mother is Nigerian, my father is Chinese-American, and for years I thought I needed to chooseassumed I had to pick a side. ①

Then lastLast summer my grandmother handed me a stack offlew in from Lagos with a folded stack of Ankara fabric scraps from Lagos and asked me to bringtake them to my studio. I had been painting oil portraits — quiet, restrained things, deliberately unplaceable.

I layered the scraps under the oil paint and we argued about it for an hour at the kitchen table. She said I was hiding them. I said I was holdingprotecting them. We were both rightNeither of us was wrong. ②

Identity, I have learned, is not a thing you pickpick once. It is a thing you keep arguing with, in the studio, at the kitchen table — in the studio, in the work itself, out loud. ③
